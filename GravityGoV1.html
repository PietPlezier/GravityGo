GravityGo

Reversi with a twist

How to Play

GravityGo is a strategic board game for two players — Reversi with a twist — played on an 8 × 8 board.

White goes first. Players take turns placing a stone of their color on an empty square.

After each placement, gravity kicks in: looking out from the placed stone in all directions (horizontal, vertical, and diagonal), stones on those lines try to slide one square toward the newly placed stone. Connected stones slide together as a chain.

Next, captures are checked. Opponent stones that are directly sandwiched between the new stone and another stone of your color (with no empty squares in between) flip to your color.

The game ends when all 64 stones have been placed. The player with the most stones of their color wins. Equal counts means a draw.

GravityGo — Full Manual

What is GravityGo?

If you know Reversi (also known as Othello), you already understand the core idea: two players take turns placing stones on a board, and you capture your opponent’s stones by sandwiching them between your own. The player with the most stones at the end wins.

GravityGo adds a powerful twist: gravity. Every time you place a stone, all surrounding stones are pulled one square toward it. This creates dramatic chain reactions that completely reshape the board every single turn.

  • The board is 8 × 8 squares. White always goes first.
  • Each player has 32 stones (64 total — the entire board fills up).
  • The game ends when all 64 stones have been placed. Most stones wins. Equal = draw.

How a Turn Works

Each turn has three phases, shown with animated highlights on the board:

  • Place — Click any empty square to place a stone. Highlights in yellow.
  • Gravity — Stones in all 8 directions slide one square toward your stone. Connected stones slide as a chain. Highlights in blue.
  • Capture — Opponent stones sandwiched between your new stone and another of your stones (no gaps) flip to your color. Highlights in red.
Important: Gravity happens before captures. Stones may shift into — or out of — capture position. Planning around this is what makes GravityGo strategic.

Game Modes

Play vs Computer

Choose your color (White or Black) and a difficulty level:

  • Easy — Focuses on capturing as many stones as possible. Predictable, good for learning.
  • Medium — Evaluates board position, preferring central control. A solid challenge.
  • Hard — Thinks multiple moves ahead. May take a few seconds early in the game.

Local Game (2 Players)

Two players share the same device, taking turns. An Undo button lets you take back moves.

Online Game

Play against someone over the internet. No account needed — the connection is direct (peer-to-peer).

If you are the host:

  • Click “Host online game”. A unique code appears (e.g. GG-AB3K).
  • Share this code with your opponent.
  • Now just wait. Do NOT click “Join” yourself — that’s for your opponent. The game starts automatically when they connect.

If you are joining:

  • Click “Join online game” and enter the code.
  • Click “Connect”. The game starts once connected.
The host plays White, the joiner plays Black. Both players must have the page open.

Turn Markers (Optional)

After each move, visual indicators appear on the stones:

  • Dot — on the stone that was just placed.
  • Arrow — on stones shifted by gravity, pointing toward the placed stone.
  • Pac-man slice — on captured stones, showing their previous color.

Use the Markers toggle above the board to turn them on or off at any time — even mid-game.

Strategy Tips

  • The center is strategically valuable — stones there influence more directions.
  • Gravity can pull opponents into capture position — but yours too!
  • Placing far from a cluster can be smarter than diving in.
  • Late-game: gravity has less effect (fewer empty squares), captures dominate.
  • Watch the stone count and “Left” numbers to gauge the tempo.
